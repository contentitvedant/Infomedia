    A. P. J. Abdul Kalam


    Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He was involved in India's space program and military missile development, earning him the title "Missile Man of India". He played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

    Early Life and Education


    Dr. Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His father was a boat owner, and his mother was a housewife. He pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, where he faced challenges but excelled. Although he aimed to be a fighter pilot, he missed selection by one rank.

    Career as a scientist

    After completing his education, Dr. Kalam joined DRDO and later moved to ISRO, where he led India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). His contributions to India's missile programs and nuclear tests were significant in strengthening the country's defense capabilities.
